RGGS PhD Dissertation Research, 2016 - Present

Marine molluskan community responses to Pliocene climate change have been studied in both the Atlantic and Pacific but the northwestern Pacific still remains unstudied. No studies have offered a quantitative analysis of community structure change or have included paleoenvironmental analyses to provide context to observed ecological changes. I propose to analyze the response of marine mollusk communities to late Pliocene warming on the coast of Claifornia and determine the environmental drivers behind observed responses using stable isotope analyses.



AMNH Research Internship, Summer 2015

I spent one month describing two new genera and species of Tritylodontidae, Shartegodon altai and Nuurtherium baruunensis, and compared them with other known tritylodontids using Morphobank and CT reconstructions.
